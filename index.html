◈ THE CONCEPT — HOW THE COUNTER TRADE WORKS
The Premise
90% of your trades go the wrong way. So this journal flips your direction automatically and tracks what would have happened if you traded the opposite. Your gut becomes your signal — inverted.
Your Trade vs Counter Trade
Your Trade = what your gut said (BUY/SELL/LONG/SHORT).
Counter Trade = the automatic flip. If you said BUY, the counter is SELL. This is what gets tracked as the primary position.
WIN / LOSS logic
Result is based on the counter trade, not yours. If price went up and you said BUY (your gut was right), the counter SELL is a LOSS. The journal is tracking whether fading you works — not whether you were right.
▤ ORDER TYPES
MARKET
Logs immediately at the current live price. No trigger needed. Use the Market BUY/SELL buttons for one-tap logging at exact market price.
LIMIT
Sets a PENDING order at a specific price level. Activates automatically when live price touches or passes through that level. BUY limits trigger when price drops to your level. SELL limits trigger when price rises to your level.
STOP
Also PENDING. Triggers on a breakout through your level — opposite of a limit. BUY stop triggers when price rises above your level. Used to enter on momentum confirmation.
TRAILING
Enters at market. The stop loss follows the price peak by a fixed distance (points or %). As the counter trade moves in your favor, the stop trails behind. If price reverses by your set distance, it closes automatically.
LADDER
Splits your entry across up to 5 price levels. Each leg activates independently when price reaches it. Combined P&L shown. Used to average into a position at multiple levels rather than one entry.
◆ P&L & PERFORMANCE TERMS
P&L pts Points
Raw price movement from entry to exit. 1 point = 1.00 in price. For forex, this is shown in pips (0.0001 movement). Sign is based on the counter trade direction — positive means the counter made money.
P&L $ Dollars
Dollar value of the P&L. Calculated as: Points × Tick Value × Contracts. Requires tick value and size to be set. GC Gold = $10/pt per contract, ES = $50/pt per contract.
MFE Max Favorable
Maximum Favorable Excursion. The furthest the counter trade moved in your favor while the trade was open — before it closed. Shows how much profit was available at peak.
MAE Max Adverse
Maximum Adverse Excursion. The furthest the counter trade moved against you while open. If MAE is large and final P&L is small, the trade was painful to hold. Useful for SL placement research.
R:R Plan Risk/Reward
Planned Risk:Reward ratio. Calculated from your entry, TP, and SL levels before the trade plays out. 1:2 means you risk 1 to make 2. Requires TP and SL to be set.
R:R Real Actual
Realized Risk:Reward. What you actually got vs what you risked. Calculated from actual exit price vs SL. A positive ratio means the counter trade exited with more profit than its risk exposure.
Favor Price Direction
Which direction price moved more during the trade — Bullish (up) or Bearish (down). Based on MFE vs MAE magnitude. If MFE > MAE, price favored the bull side more.
Conviction 1–5
How strongly your gut felt about the trade at entry. 1 = weak feeling, 5 = extremely certain. Tracked over time to see if high-conviction gut feelings lose more often — which would mean the counter trade wins more on those.
⬡ ADVANCED TRADE STATS (Journal Expand)
Drawdown % Risk
MAE expressed as a percentage of your entry price. Example: entry at 4500, MAE = -9pts = 0.2% drawdown. Useful for comparing drawdown severity across different assets and price levels.
Time in Drawdown Duration
Approximate time the counter trade spent below entry (in losing territory). Estimated from total duration × MAE ratio. Long drawdown time = painful hold. Suggests your SL may be too loose or entry timing is off.
Efficiency % Quality
How much of the available profit (MFE) you captured at exit.
100% = exited exactly at the best point.
50% = left half the move on the table.
Negative = you gave back all profit and closed at a loss after being up.
Edge Ratio MFE ÷ MAE
MFE divided by MAE. Above 1.0 = price moved further in your favor than against — the trade had positive edge. Below 1.0 = the trade fought you more than it rewarded you. A consistently low edge ratio means your entries are poorly timed.
Counter Edge Delta
Net point difference between the counter trade P&L and your original direction P&L. Positive = being flipped was better than following your gut. Negative = your gut was right this time. Over many trades this shows the average advantage of fading yourself.
MFE/MAE Bar Visual
Horizontal bar showing what % of total price movement went in the counter's favor (green) vs against it (red). A mostly green bar = the trade worked cleanly. Mostly red = price fought the counter throughout.
Lesson Panel Auto
Automatically generated observations based on your trade data. Flags patterns like: early exit, deep drawdown with no SL, high conviction + loss (gut trap), gave back profit. Read these — they compound into pattern recognition over time.
Row Color Visual
Expanded rows have a subtle green tint when the counter trade won and a red tint when it lost. Instant visual context without reading every cell.
◉ TRADE RESULTS
WIN
Counter trade closed profitably. Price moved in the counter direction. You manually closed at a profit, or TP was hit.
LOSS
Counter trade closed at a loss. Price moved in your original direction (your gut was right this time). Closed manually at a loss, or SL was hit.
TP
Take Profit hit. Counter trade automatically closed when price reached the TP level. Result = WIN.
SL
Stop Loss hit. Counter trade automatically closed when price hit the SL level. Result = LOSS.
OPEN
Trade is currently active. Live P&L is updating in real time based on current market price vs entry.
PENDING
Limit or Stop order waiting for price to reach the trigger level. Not yet active — no P&L until activated.
📊 CHART REPLAY
Blue arrow / line
Your entry. The price you entered the trade at and the direction your gut said. Arrow points up for BUY/LONG, down for SELL/SHORT.
Green / Red circle
Exit point. Green circle = counter trade WON at close. Red circle = counter trade LOST at close. Labeled CTR: WIN or CTR: LOSS.
Dashed blue line
Entry price level. Horizontal line across the chart at your exact entry price for reference.
Solid green line
Take Profit level. Where the counter trade is targeting. Only shows if TP was set.
Solid red line
Stop Loss level. Where the counter trade gets stopped out. Only shows if SL was set.
Dotted green line
MFE level. The highest price the counter trade reached in its favor. Shows how close it got to the ideal exit.
Dotted red line
MAE level. The worst price the counter trade reached against you. Shows the maximum drawdown while open.
